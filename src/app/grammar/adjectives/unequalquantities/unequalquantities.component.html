{{ 'GRAMMAR.UnequalQuantities' | translate }}

When comparing two unequal things, we should use the following structure:

adjective indicating quantity + noun + than

Note that adjective used here may depend on whether the noun is countable or uncountable. If the noun is countable, then we should use more or fewer. Whereas, if the noun in uncountable, we should use more or less.

Examples:

  • I have more water than he does. - "water" is an uncountable noun
  • I have more bottles than he does. - "bottles" is a countable noun
  • San Francisco has less traffic than New York City. - "traffic" is an uncountable noun
  • San Francisco has fewer cars than New York City.- "cars" is a countable noun
