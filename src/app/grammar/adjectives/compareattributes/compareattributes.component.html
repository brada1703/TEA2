{{ 'GRAMMAR.CompareAttributes' | translate }}

When comparing attributes, we have two main sentence structures. When the attributes are equal we use the following structure:

as + comparative adjective + as

Examples:

  • John is as fast as me.
  • I am as smart as she is.
  • Lucy is as pleasant as Samantha.

When the attributes are unequal, we have three different possible sentence structures:

We can express simply that the two attributes are unequal:

not as + comparative adjective + as

We can also show that something is less of an attribute than another:

less + comparative adjective + than

Finally, we can simply use the adjective and than.

comparative adjective + than

Examples:

  • The Yellow River is not as long as the Nile.
  • The Yellow River is shorter than the Nile.
  • My teacher is less forgiving than yours.
  • My teacher is not as forgiving as yours.
  • Video games are less important than grammar practice.
  • Grammar practice is more important than playing video games.
  • Video games are not as important as grammar practice.
  • Portugal is sunnier than England.
  • England is less sunny than Portugal.
