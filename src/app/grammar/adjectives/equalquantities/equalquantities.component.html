{{ 'GRAMMAR.EqualQuantities' | translate }}

When comparing two things of equal quantity, we use the following structure:

as + adjective + (noun) + as

Note that the adjective will depend on whether the noun is countable or uncountable. For countable nouns, we can use many and few. Whereas, for uncountable nouns, we should use much and little.

Examples:

  • I have as many bottles ashe does. - countable
  • I have as much water ashe does. - uncountable
  • They have as much time as we do. - uncountable
  • They have as many hours in the day as we do. - countable
  • Sara wants as few tasks as I have.- countable
  • Sara wants as little work as I have. - uncountable
