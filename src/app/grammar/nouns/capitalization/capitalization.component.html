{{ 'GRAMMAR.Capitalization' | translate }}

Capital letters are used with certain types of nouns, in certain positions in the sentence, and with some adjectives. One must always capitalize letters for:

Sentence Location:

The first word in the sentence is always capitalized.

Examples:

  • He ran home.
  • They live in the city.

People and works of art:

The pronoun for the first person, singular, I, is always capitalized.

Examples:

  • He and I ran home.
  • They live in the city. Yet, I live in the countryside.

The names and titles of people are capitalized.

Examples:

  • Barack Obama
  • Anne Frank
  • Julius Ceasar
  • the Queen of England
  • the Prime Minister of Canada

The titles of movies, books, and piece of art are capitalized.

Examples:

  • The Wizard of Oz
  • It's a Wonderful Life
  • Pride and Prejudice
  • The Great Gatsby
  • The Great Wave off Kanagawa
  • The Course of Empire

Nationalities and Languages:

All country names, their adjectives, collective nouns for nationalities, and language names are capitalized.

Examples:

  • France
  • Germany
  • China
  • Mexican food
  • Angolan music
  • Brazilian dance
  • the Australians
  • the Indians
  • the South Africans
  • I speak Cantonese.
  • He speaks Dutch.
  • We understand Spanish.

Geography:

The names of streets, buildings, parks, districts, villages, towns, cities, states, provinces, rivers, lakes, seas, oceans, and geographical formations are capitalized.

Examples:

  • Laura Street
  • First Lane
  • Sydney Opera House
  • Chrysler Building
  • Central Park
  • Grand View Park
  • Castro District
  • Mission District
  • Jeffersonville
  • Almond Village
  • Deep River
  • Cobalt
  • New York City
  • San Francisco
  • Texas
  • Florida
  • Ontario
  • Alberta
  • Mississippi River
  • Ganges River
  • Lake Ontario
  • Lake Michigan
  • Red Sea
  • Caribbean Sea
  • Atlantic Ocean
  • Pacific Ocean
  • Mount Everest
  • Sahara Desert

Time:

All days, holidays, months, and seasons are capitalized.

Examples:

  • Monday
  • Friday
  • Christmas
  • Passover
  • January
  • March
  • Spring
  • Fall
