{{ 'GRAMMAR.PluralNouns' | translate }}

The plural form of the noun in most cases is formed by simply adding an "-s" to the end of the word.


Regular Nouns:

Singular Plural
apple apples
chair chairs
book books
computer computers

Irregular Nouns:

Unfortunately, there are a lot of exceptions in English. However, some of these have rules. In the following examples, to pluralize singular nouns ending in the letters, s, x, z, ch, and sh simply add -es.

Singular Plural
box boxes
wish wishes
watch watches
kiss kisses
buzz buzzes

For nouns ending in -y, we simply drop the -y and add -ies.

Singular Plural
baby babies
hobby hobbies
family families
berry berries
lady ladies

Often, words ending in the letter -f will drop the -f and add -ves to the end of the word.

Singular Plural
leaf leaves
half halves
knife knives
wife wives
life lives
elf elves
loaf loaves

The English language takes some nouns from Latin and uses their grammar to make them plural. In most instances, when the words, originating from Latin, end in -us will drop the -us and add -i to the end of the word.

Singular Plural
cactus cacti
focus foci
fungus fungi
nucleus nuclei
syllabus syllabi

Words that end in -is often drop the -is and add -es to the end of the word.

Singular Plural
analysis analyses
diagnosis diagnoses
oasis oases
thesis theses
crisis crises

Unfortunately, there are nouns that don't follow any rules. The most common ones are below.

Singular Plural
woman women
man men
child children
tooth teeth
foot feet
person people
mouse mice
goose geese
potato potatoes
tomato tomatoes
phenomenon phenomena
criterion criteria
datum data

Some words are the same in singular and plural.

Singular Plural
aircraft aircraft
spacecraft spacecraft
deer deer
fish fish
moose moose
sheep sheep
shrimp shrimp
