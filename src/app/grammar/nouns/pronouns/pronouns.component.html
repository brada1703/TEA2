{{ 'GRAMMAR.Pronouns' | translate }}

Pronouns take the place of a noun. We use pronouns mainly to avoid repeating the subject in the sentence. Which pronoun we use will depend on how the pronoun is used in the sentence.


Subject Pronouns

Pronouns can be used as a subject in the sentence.

Person Number Gender Pronoun Translation
First Singular Neutral I yo
Second Singular Neutral you tu / usted
Third Singular Masculine he el
Third Singular Feminine she ella
Third Singular Neutral it (no existe)
First Plural Neutral we nosotros
Second Plural Neutral you ustedes
Third Plural Neutral they ellos/ellas

  • I am 26 years old.
  • You are my friend.
  • John is from England. He is English.
  • Jane is from Ireland. She is Irish.
  • We are from Canada.
  • You are the best students I have ever had.
  • They don't want to leave class.

Object Pronouns

Pronouns can also be used as the object of a preposition or a clause. Notice that "you" and "it" stay the same regardless of whether they are the subject or an object.

Person Number Gender Pronoun Translation
First Singular Neutral me mi
Second Singular Neutral you ti / usted
Third Singular Masculine him el
Third Singular Feminine her ella
Third Singular Neutral it (no existe)
First Plural Neutral us nosotros
Second Plural Neutral you ustedes
Third Plural Neutral them ellos/ellas

  • Give me the pencil!
  • I will hand you a piece of paper.
  • Give it to him!
  • John, pass the ball to your sister. Pass it to her.
  • The puppy wants to play. Give it a ball.
  • We would like some water. Give us some water, please.
  • You are the best students! I will give you all an A!
  • They are also working very hard. I will give them all an A.

Possessive Pronouns (Adjectives)

Pronouns can also be used as adjectives to express ownership.

Person Number Gender Pronoun Translation
First Singular Neutral my mi(s)
Second Singular Neutral your tu(s)
Third Singular Masculine his su(s)
Third Singular Feminine her su(s)
Third Singular Neutral its su(s)
First Plural Neutral our nuestr(-a,-os,-as)
Second Plural Neutral your vuestro(-a,-os,-as)
Third Plural Neutral their su(s)

  • It is my pencil!
  • That is your piece of paper.
  • Is it his dog?
  • She has a dog. It's her dog.
  • The puppy has a ball somewhere. Is that it's ball?
  • We would like some water. Is that our water?
  • You are the best students! I can really see that your work has paid off!
  • They are also working very hard. Their enthusiasm is contagious!

Possessive Pronouns (Nouns)

Instead of using the possessive pronoun as an adjective and a noun, we can replace this with the possessive pronoun that is used as a noun.

Person Number Gender Pronoun Translation
First Singular Neutral mine mio(s)/mia(s)
Second Singular Neutral yours tuyo(s)/tuya(s)
Third Singular Masculine his suyo(s)/suya(s)
Third Singular Feminine hers suyo(s)/suya(s)
Third Singular Neutral (does not exist) suyo(s)/suya(s)
First Plural Neutral ours nuestro(s)/nuestra(s)
Second Plural Neutral yours vuestro(s)/vuestra(s)
Third Plural Neutral theirs suyo(s)/suya(s)

  • It is my pencil! It's mine!
  • That is your piece of paper. It's yours!
  • Is it his dog? It's his!
  • She has a dog. It's her dog. It's hers!
  • We would like some water. Is that our water? It's ours!
  • Does everyone want a cookie? If so, then they are yours!
  • Do they want to finish my ice cream? If so, it is theirs.

Reflexive Pronouns

We use reflexive pronouns when the subject of the clause is the same as the object. Remember that only certain verbs can be reflexive.

Person Number Gender Pronoun Translation
First Singular Neutral myself me
Second Singular Neutral yourself te
Third Singular Masculine himself se
Third Singular Feminine herself se
Third Singular Neutral itself se
First Plural Neutral ourselves nos
Second Plural Neutral yourselves se
Third Plural Neutral themselves se

  • I did it to myself.
  • You did it to yourself.
  • He did it to himself.
  • She did it to herself.
  • It did it to itself.
  • We did it to ourselves.
  • You did it to yourselves.
  • They did it to themselves.
